Couples Therapy

Couples therapy isn't just for when things feel like they're falling apart, it can also be a space to better understand each other and your relationship.

It offers a structured, neutral environment where both partners can speak openly, feel heard, and work through challenges together. This might include communication difficulties, recurring conflicts, trust concerns, emotional distance, or navigating important life decisions.

The focus isn't on "who's right," but on understanding what's happening between you and finding ways to move forward that feel healthier and more supportive for both of you.

Whether you're looking to repair, reconnect, or simply gain clarity, therapy can help you have conversations that are often hard to have on your own.

Confidentiality

What you share in therapy stays private.

Confidentiality is a core part of the process, it helps create a space where you can speak honestly and safely.

There are a few exceptions, which we'll always be transparent about:

  • If there is a risk of harm to yourself or someone else
  • If there is concern about abuse or safety
  • If required by law

In these situations, the priority is safety, and only the necessary information is shared. If you ever have questions about confidentiality, you're always welcome to ask, we'll walk you through it.
